Course Description

Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning (AI/ML) are rapidly transforming pharmacometrics, offering new tools for model development, covariate selection, and data analysis. Organized by the AI/ML Special Interest Group (SIG) under ISoP, this interactive satellite workshop provides both an overview and hands-on experience with AI-assisted coding tools and open-source ML frameworks in R and Julia, alongside a panel discussion of regulatory considerations and best practices.

🤖

GenAI Coding Assistants

Hands-on demonstration of AI coding tools including GitHub Copilot to accelerate pharmacometric workflows.

💻

Live Coding Exercises

Open-source exercises in R and Julia covering Neural ODEs, ML-based covariate selection, and Explainable AI.

⚖️

Regulatory Panel

Discussion with regulatory and industry experts on AI/ML best practices and submission-readiness.

👥

Networking

Connect and build community with peers working at the intersection of AI/ML and pharmacometrics.

Prerequisites & Logistics

  • Participants must bring their own laptop
  • A GitHub account is required for the hands-on exercises
  • Basic familiarity with R or Julia is helpful but not required

Agenda

A full day of talks, hands-on coding sessions, and expert discussions. The program is outlined below and will be updated as the event approaches.

Morning
08:30 – 09:00
Welcome & Introductions Logistics

Registration, coffee, and opening remarks from the AI/ML SIG organizers.

09:00 – 10:00
Overview of AI/ML in Pharmacometrics Lecture

Landscape overview: where AI/ML methods stand today and where they are heading in drug development and regulatory science.

10:00 – 11:00
Hands-on: GenAI Coding Assistants Hands-on

Interactive demonstration of GitHub Copilot and other GenAI tools applied to pharmacometric coding tasks.

11:00 – 11:15
Mid-Morning Coffee Break
11:15 – 12:30
Hands-on: Neural ODEs for PK/PD Modeling Hands-on

Live coding in Julia/R — building and fitting Neural ODE models for pharmacokinetic data.

12:30 – 13:30
Light Lunch & Networking
Afternoon
13:30 – 14:30
Hands-on: ML-Based Covariate Selection Hands-on
